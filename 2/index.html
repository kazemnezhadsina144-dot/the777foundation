Volunteer & Collaborate

Help build a survivor-led, trauma-informed support system for newcomers and marginalized workers in British Columbia.

Who we are

The 777 Foundation Society is a survivor-led, trauma-informed non-profit in British Columbia. We design and deliver peer-led programs, legal advocacy support, emotional resilience activities, and outreach initiatives for newcomers and marginalized workers facing unsafe or exclusionary conditions.

We are in an early pilot and capacity-building phase and are intentionally building a small, stable cohort of volunteers, professional allies, and connectors who want to help shape a safer, more dignified path through BC’s systems – without exploiting anyone’s story.

What we’re building

  • Peer-led groups and survivor-informed support networks
  • Legal advocacy orientation and system navigation (not representation)
  • Emotional resilience practices to reduce isolation and burnout
  • Story-based outreach that highlights patterns of harm while protecting identities

Programs are being developed carefully and at small scale so we can document what works, refine our methods, and grow in a safe, sustainable way.

Volunteer & collaboration pathways

At this stage, we’re prioritising a few focused ways to get involved. Most roles are 2–4 hours per month, remote-friendly, with a preference for BC-based volunteers and partners.

  • Funding, Grants & Partnerships Steward – channel aligned funding, grants, and partnerships into the organisation so we can build stable programs and infrastructure.
  • Legal, Policy & Advocacy Allies – advise on content and navigation frameworks for key systems.
  • Mental Health & Social Service Allies – support trauma-informed and culturally responsive practice and referrals.
  • Community Organisers, Designers & Storytellers – help with outreach, education materials, and pattern-level advocacy.
  • Non-profit Operations & Governance Support – help strengthen policies, evaluation, reporting, and board tools.

Featured role: Funding, Grants & Partnerships Steward

This role exists to open and stabilise the resource flow into The 777 Foundation Society. You help translate our survivor-led mandate into concrete funding, grant, and partnership opportunities – and build the bridges so that aligned institutions, community foundations, family offices, and donors can support our work in a clean, structured way.

What you’ll focus on

  • Identifying aligned funding pathways (grants, community foundations, public programs, and select philanthropic or family-office opportunities).
  • Helping prioritise a small number of high-fit opportunities instead of chasing everything.
  • Acting as a connector and translator between our survivor-led story and the language of funders and institutions.
  • Coordinating with our core team so proposals are realistic and stay trauma-informed and dignity-first.
  • Where appropriate, supporting early-stage partnership conversations with community organisations and allies.

What this role is not

  • Not a solo fundraiser expected to “bring in money” alone.
  • Not responsible for legal or financial sign-off.
  • Not a public spokesperson for lived-experience stories.
  • Not an emergency or crisis-response role.

Who this might suit

  • Experience in grants, fundraising, partnerships, social finance or non-profit management.
  • Strong alignment with survivor-led, trauma-informed, and dignity-focused work.
  • Comfortable reading funding guidelines and quickly spotting fit / misfit.
  • Able to offer realistic, steady time (for example 2–4 hours per month over at least 6 months).

How to express interest

If you’d like to explore volunteering or collaborating with The 777 Foundation Society – especially in a funding, grants, or partnership capacity – please send a short email.

Email: contact@the777foundation.org

Suggested structure for your message:

  • Who you are (1–2 lines: role, location, relevant experience).
  • Why this kind of work matters to you and how you imagine contributing.
  • Your realistic availability (e.g. 2–4 hours/month).

Please don’t include sensitive personal details or case-specific information in your first message. If there’s a good fit, we can set up a time to talk and decide on next steps together.
