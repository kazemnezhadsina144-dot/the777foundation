Featured role (volunteer/advisory)

Funding, Grants & Partnerships Steward

Purpose: open and stabilise the resource flow into The 777 Foundation Society. Translate the survivor-led mandate into concrete funding, grant, and partnership opportunities, and build bridges so aligned institutions and donors can support the work in a clean, structured way.

2–4 hrs / month to start Remote-friendly Outcomes > activity

What this role focuses on

  • Identify aligned pathways (grants, community foundations, municipal/provincial/federal programs, select philanthropy) that match mandate and scale.
  • Prioritise a small number of high-fit opportunities instead of chasing everything.
  • Act as connector/translator between survivor-led mandate and funder language (LOIs, concept notes, short applications).
  • Coordinate with the core team to keep proposals realistic for current capacity and dignity-first delivery.
  • Where appropriate, support early-stage partnership conversations with community organisations, clinics, advocacy groups, and educational institutions.

What this role is not

  • Not a solo fundraiser expected to “bring in money” without structure or support.
  • Not responsible for legal or financial sign-off.
  • Not a public spokesperson required to share lived-experience details.
  • Not a crisis-response or frontline counselling role.

Organisation

The 777 Foundation Society — emerging non-profit society based in British Columbia, Canada. Mission: protect dignity and create long-term opportunity for people navigating vulnerable transitions, especially newcomers and workers under high economic or psychological pressure.

Role summary

This is not a general grant-search role. The Funding & Partnerships Lead owns a small, serious pipeline and turns the Society’s mandate into fundable offers and clean partnerships, while the founder remains in the architect/strategy role.

What you will own
  • Design and manage a realistic funding pipeline (prioritised opportunities with clear stages and next actions).
  • Initiate and manage external relationships (funders, institutions, aligned partners) through to clear outcomes (yes/no/later).
  • Translate program intent into fundable concepts (LOIs, short proposals), coordinating with the core team for feasibility and dignity-first delivery.
  • Provide simple reporting: stage, next step, probability, and time-to-decision. Measured on movement and results.
What success looks like (12 months)
  • Net new funding secured (illustrative target: CAD 75k–150k) via grants, institutional funders, or aligned major donors.
  • Several concrete wins (illustrative target: 3–6 approved grants/contributions/commitments).
  • Live, realistic pipeline (illustrative: ~10–20 active opportunities with stages: research → contact → proposal → review → decision).
  • Quarterly forward motion: multiple opportunities advance at least one stage per quarter.
Compensation & structure

Phase 1 (3–6 months): lean start (volunteer/advisory or small retainer by agreement) + success-based bonuses tied to funds actually received by the Society. Phase 2: evolves to stable part-time leadership + performance bonus if milestones are achieved and fit is strong.

How we start
  • Align on scope and a 3–6 month pilot.
  • Pick a small number of priority targets.
  • You lead external motion; core team supports narrative/materials and decisions.
  • Regular check-ins; adjust based on what works in reality.

How involvement works

  1. Short email (role + interest + realistic availability).
  2. Routing to the right pathway (funding, legal/policy, mental health, outreach, operations).
  3. Quick fit check (asynchronous first; short call only if needed).
  4. Start small with one defined contribution.
  5. Review after 4–8 weeks and adjust scope.

The goal is sustainable contribution without burnout, and without extracting anyone’s story.

Boundaries and safety

  • No crisis-response or emergency service.
  • No legal representation; no clinical therapy delivery.
  • Privacy-first: avoid sensitive identifiers in first contact.
  • Consent-first storytelling: pattern-level outreach, identity-protected.
  • Clear scope protects participants, volunteers, and partners.

Detailed policies are available under Privacy and Scope.
