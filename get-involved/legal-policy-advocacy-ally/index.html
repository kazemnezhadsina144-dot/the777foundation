This role does not provide legal advice to the public or represent individuals. The focus is education, readiness, and safe routing.

1) About the Society

The 777 Foundation Society is a survivor-led, trauma-informed non-profit in British Columbia building peer-led support, advocacy orientation (not representation), resilience practices, and privacy-first outreach for newcomers and marginalized workers.

2) Role summary

This role strengthens the Society’s “system navigation layer”: plain-language guides, documentation readiness, and referral pathways. Work is measured by clarity, safety, and usefulness — not volume.

3) What you will support

Plain-language frameworks

  • Review and improve rights/process explanations (e.g., employment standards, human rights, workers’ compensation).
  • Reduce complexity without overpromising outcomes.
  • Standardize “what to prepare” checklists (evidence, timelines, forms).

Documentation readiness

  • Help structure safe templates: incident timelines, evidence indexes, and meeting notes.
  • Promote privacy-first redaction and “minimum necessary” disclosure.
  • Support “next step” routing to existing services when required.

Referral pathways

  • Map reputable community/legal resources and when to use them.
  • Clarify boundaries: education + readiness, not advice/representation.
  • Improve participant safety and reduce wasted motion.

Ethics & risk discipline

  • Help ensure materials do not become “legal advice” or unsafe directives.
  • Keep language conservative and defensible.
  • Align with the Society’s privacy and safeguarding policies.
4) What this role is not
  • No legal representation.
  • No individual legal advice to the public.
  • No crisis response.
  • No requirement to be public-facing or disclose lived experience.
5) What success looks like (first 8–12 weeks)
Core outputs

2–4 concise, plain-language “navigation sheets” + 2 templates (timeline + evidence index) with safety notes.

Safety & clarity

Clear scope disclaimers and routing guidance embedded across materials.

Resource map

A vetted referral list (BC-focused) with “when to refer” criteria.
