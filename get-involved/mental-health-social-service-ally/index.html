Note: Not an emergency service. If immediate danger or acute distress, contact local emergency/crisis services.

1) About the work

The Society provides peer-led spaces and practical system literacy for people under stress from unsafe or exploitative conditions. This ally role strengthens safety and care quality without shifting the Society into a clinical service.

2) Role summary

Help design safety boundaries, facilitation guardrails, referral guidance, and basic response protocols for non-clinical peer programs. Support cultural responsiveness, consent-first practice, and realistic pacing.

3) What you will own

Trauma-informed guardrails

  • Session safety norms and facilitation boundaries.
  • Consent-first disclosure guidance.
  • Burnout prevention and pacing recommendations.

Safeguarding & escalation

  • Non-clinical response scripts (what to do / what not to do).
  • Clear escalation/referral steps for risk scenarios.
  • Privacy-first notes and minimal data principles.

Referral pathways

  • Local resource mapping (BC focus) + remote options.
  • Warm handoff templates (non-identifying initial steps).
  • Simple “where to go next” guides.

Quality review

  • Review program drafts for safety and realism.
  • Identify scope creep into clinical territory.
  • Short recommendations and change log.
4) Boundaries
  • No clinical therapy delivery within the Society.
  • No crisis-response service (the Society routes out).
  • Focus is guardrails, referrals, and non-clinical support quality.
5) Who this fits
  • Registered clinicians, counsellors, social workers, or experienced community mental health practitioners.
  • Comfort with non-clinical community settings and strong boundary discipline.
  • Ability to produce simple, usable guidance (not academic reports).
6) How we start
  1. Pick one pilot stream to review (peer-led groups or resilience activities).
  2. Define safeguarding minimums and referral flow.
  3. Deliver one iteration cycle, then expand gradually.
7) Next step

Email a short note with background, preferred support area, and availability. Avoid sensitive identifiers in first contact.
