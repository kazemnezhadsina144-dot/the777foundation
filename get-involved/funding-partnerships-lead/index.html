1) About The 777 Foundation Society

The 777 Foundation Society is in an early build phase. The mission is to design and run programs that protect dignity and create long-term opportunity for people navigating vulnerable transitions — especially newcomers, workers in unstable conditions, and those under high economic or psychological pressure.

Current focus
  • Governance and legal structure
  • Program architecture (pilot designs)
  • Funding pathways (public, philanthropic, and strategic partners)
Next phase

Public-facing programs and member growth start once core funding and infrastructure are in place.

2) Role summary

This is not a general “grant search” or advisory role. The Funding & Partnerships Lead actively brings external resources into the Society and owns the early pipeline from identification to clear outcome (yes / no / later). The role sits between the Society and funders/partners, while the founder remains in the architect/strategy role.

3) What you will own

Funding pipeline ownership

  • Identify and prioritise realistic funding sources (government programs, foundations, institutional funders, major donors, aligned partners).
  • Maintain a small but serious pipeline (not a large list of theoretical options).
  • Keep stages clear: research → contact → proposal → review → decision.

External relationship management

  • Act as the primary point of contact for early-stage discussions.
  • Initiate contact, schedule conversations, and follow through consistently.
  • Maintain professional follow-up until a clear outcome exists.

Shaping proposals & offers

  • Translate the Society’s vision into fundable proposals and partnership concepts.
  • Co-design narrative/materials with the founder, then tailor per funder requirements.
  • Protect feasibility and dignity-first delivery (scope discipline).

Reporting & accountability

  • Short updates: opportunities, stage, next step, probability, and outcomes.
  • Time spent must be justified by impact and probability.
  • Measured on movement and results, not volume of documents.
4) What success looks like (first 12 months)
Funding secured

Meaningful net new funding (illustrative: CAD 75k–150k) via grants, institutional funders, or major donors.

Concrete wins

Several approvals/commitments (illustrative: 3–6 contributions or multi-year commitments).

Healthy pipeline

A live pipeline of ~10–20 active opportunities with clear stages and next actions.

Quarterly motion

Multiple opportunities advance at least one full step each quarter.

5) Compensation & structure

Phase 1 — Pilot (first 3–6 months)

  • Lean start (volunteer/advisory or small retainer by agreement).
  • Success-based bonuses tied directly to funds actually received by the Society.
  • Principle: rewards follow real resource movement, not activity.

Phase 2 — After initial milestones

  • Part-time leadership position with stable base pay.
  • Performance bonus linked to total funds raised over a year.
6) Who you are
  • Comfortable being measured on outcomes (funding, commitments, concrete progress).
  • Skilled at relationship-building with decision-makers (government, philanthropy, institutions).
  • Can work with a founder-architect: translate strategy into fundable offers without hand-holding.
  • Organised and persistent; maintains a small pipeline and follows through.
  • Values dignity, fairness, and system-level impact.

Experience is a plus (nonprofit development, fundraising, grant writing, institutional partnerships, philanthropy, impact program design) but not strictly required.

7) How we’ll start
  1. Align on scope, expectations, and initial targets for a 3–6 month pilot.
  2. Define a small number of priority funding/partnership targets.
  3. You lead the external motion; the founder supports on strategy and program architecture.
  4. Review progress regularly and adjust based on what is actually working.
