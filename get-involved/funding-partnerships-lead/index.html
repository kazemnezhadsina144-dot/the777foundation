1) About The 777 Foundation Society

The Society is in an early build phase. Mission: design and run programs that protect dignity and create long-term opportunity for people navigating vulnerable transitions — especially newcomers and workers facing unstable conditions or high economic/psychological pressure.

Current focus
  • Governance and legal structure
  • Program architecture (pilot designs)
  • Funding pathways (public, philanthropic, and strategic partners)

Public-facing programs and member growth start once core funding and operating infrastructure are in place.

Role at a glance

  • External title: Funding & Partnerships Lead
  • Core function: bring resources + build bridges to aligned funders/partners
  • Scope: small but serious pipeline (not a big theoretical list)
  • Founder role: architect/strategy support; role leads external motion

This is not a general “grant search” role.

2) Role summary

The Funding & Partnerships Lead actively brings external resources into the Society and owns the early funding and partnership pipeline from identification to clear outcome (yes / no / later). The role sits between the Society and funders, institutions, and aligned partners.

3) What you will own

A) Funding pipeline design & ownership

  • Identify and prioritise realistic sources (government programs, foundations, institutional funders, major donors, aligned partners).
  • Maintain a focused pipeline with stages and next actions (not a large list of links).

B) External relationship building & management

  • Act as primary point of contact for early-stage funder/partner discussions.
  • Initiate outreach, support meetings where needed, and run consistent follow-up to clear outcomes.

C) Shaping proposals & opportunities

  • Translate program vision into fundable concepts and partnership offers.
  • Coordinate with the core team; tailor submissions to funder requirements.

D) Reporting & accountability

  • Regular updates: opportunity, stage, next step, probability, and outcome.
  • Effort must be justified by potential impact and likelihood.

Measurement: movement + results, not the number of documents produced.

What this role is not

  • Not “just grant research”
  • Not legal sign-off or financial sign-off
  • Not public storytelling based on lived experience
  • Not crisis response or frontline counselling

4) What success looks like (first 12 months)

Funding secured

Meaningful net new funding (illustrative: CAD 75k–150k) via grants, institutional funders, or major donors.

Concrete wins

Several approvals/commitments (illustrative: 3–6 grants, signed contributions, or multi-year commitments).

Healthy pipeline

A live pipeline (illustrative: 10–20 active opportunities) with clear stages and next steps.

Quarterly motion: multiple opportunities advance at least one stage each quarter.

5) Compensation & structure

Phase 1 — Pilot (3–6 months)

  • Lean start (volunteer/advisory or small retainer by agreement).
  • Success-based bonuses tied to funds actually received by the Society.
  • Principle: paid when real resources move, not for activity alone.

Phase 2 — After milestones

  • Part-time leadership with stable base pay.
  • Performance bonus linked to total funds raised over a year.

6) Who you are

  • Comfortable being measured on outcomes (funding, commitments, concrete progress).
  • Skilled at relationship-building with government, philanthropy, or institutional decision-makers.
  • Can translate strategy into fundable offers with minimal hand-holding.
  • Organised and persistent with follow-through.
  • Aligned with dignity, fairness, and system-level impact.

Helpful experience (not required): nonprofit development, grant writing, institutional partnerships, philanthropy, impact program design.

7) How we’ll start

  1. Align on scope, expectations, and a 3–6 month pilot.
  2. Define a small set of priority targets.
  3. You lead external motion; the core team supports strategy and materials.
  4. Review progress regularly and adjust based on what works in reality.
