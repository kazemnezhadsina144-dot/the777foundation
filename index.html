Stories, cases & Code 777

We publish carefully anonymised, trauma-aware versions of real cases – not to expose people, but to expose patterns. Those patterns feed Code 777: our way of turning pain into structured learning.

In the public version of Code 777, we:

  • Describe real experiences in plain language, with all identifiers removed.
  • Map each case against processes like ESB, WSBC, HRT, Ombudsperson, and immigration.
  • Extract patterns: where systems protect people – and where they silently fail.

Case publishing is paused while we build safety, consent, and governance layers. Until then, we focus on internal pattern-tracking and education.

Trust, structure & language

We are building slowly and deliberately: legally grounded, ethics-driven, and transparent about what we can and cannot do.

How we’re structured

  • Registered as a non-profit society in British Columbia (The 777 Foundation Society).
  • Based in Vancouver, BC – piloting programs with a focus on newcomers and workers.
  • Independent from political parties and tied to no single employer, union, or agency.
  • Committed to trauma-aware, faith-safe, and culturally sensitive practice.
