Stories, cases & Code 777

We work with real experiences and patterns, not abstract theory. Code 777 is our internal way of turning lived cases into structured learning without exposing individuals.

In the public side of Code 777, we aim to:

  • Describe types of cases in plain language, with all identifiers removed.
  • Map what people went through across systems like ESB, WSBC, HRT, Ombudsperson, and immigration.
  • Highlight where processes worked, where they broke down, and what might be improved.

Public case summaries will only be published once we have clear consent processes, privacy safeguards, and governance oversight in place.

Trust, structure and accountability

We are building slowly and deliberately: legally grounded, ethically careful, and transparent about both our strengths and limits.

How we’re structured

  • Registered as a non-profit society in British Columbia (The 777 Foundation Society).
  • Based in Vancouver, BC, with a focus on newcomers and workers in the province.
  • Governed by a volunteer board of directors and guided by external advisors as we grow.
  • Committed to privacy-conscious, trauma-informed, and culturally aware practice.

As our structure develops, we will publish our board list, key policies, and short annual impact notes here.

What we can and cannot do

  • We can listen, help you organise your story, and point you toward formal processes.
  • We can offer basic rights information and connect you with appropriate services where possible.
  • We cannot provide legal representation, immigration consulting, or emergency crisis response.
  • We will always be honest about our capacity and refer out when we are not the right fit.

Our goal is to be a clear, steady bridge – not to replace unions, clinics, or emergency services.
