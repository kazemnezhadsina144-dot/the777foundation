Survivor-led non-profit • British Columbia

A grassroots foundation for dignity and justice in the lives of newcomers and marginalized workers.

The 777 Foundation Society is a survivor-led, trauma-informed non-profit in British Columbia. We build peer-led support networks, advocacy orientation (not legal representation), emotional resilience practices, and privacy-first outreach for people navigating vulnerable transitions and systemic mistreatment.

What we do

Structured support that reduces isolation and improves readiness

  • Peer-led groups designed for safety, boundaries, and practical next steps.
  • Plain-language system literacy and documentation readiness support.
  • Resilience practices coordinated with existing community resources.
  • Outreach that highlights patterns while protecting identities and consent.

Program streams

The Society’s work is organized into four connected streams designed to stabilize, inform, and support safe next steps.

Stream 1

Peer-led Programs

Facilitated peer-led groups with clear boundaries, safety practices, and practical guidance.

Stream 2

Advocacy Orientation

Documentation readiness and system navigation support for engaging formal processes (not legal representation).

Stream 3

Emotional Resilience

Simple resilience practices and tools to reduce isolation and strengthen coping capacity.

Stream 4

Community Outreach

Privacy-first education and awareness initiatives focused on patterns, not exposure of individuals.
