A grassroots foundation for dignity, safety, and justice for newcomers and marginalized workers.

We provide trauma-informed, peer-led support and rights navigation for people facing exploitation, exclusion, and system stress. Our focus is practical: stabilise, document, understand options, and move forward safely.

Who we serve

  • Newcomers and migrant workers navigating unsafe or exploitative conditions.
  • People experiencing rights confusion, retaliation fears, or isolation while seeking help.
  • Individuals interacting with complaint systems (employment, human rights, compensation, etc.).

What we do (in plain language)

  • Peer-led groups with boundaries and facilitation (not unstructured disclosure).
  • Rights navigation and documentation readiness (not legal representation).
  • Resilience tools to reduce isolation and improve coping during system processes.

  • Connection: reduced isolation via safe peer network and referrals.
  • Stability: basic coping plan for system stress (sleep, routines, supports).
